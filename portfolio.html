    • Deferred Renderer

      • I created an OpenGL 3D graphics engine that uses the advanced technique of Deferred Rendering. In my deferred renderer, the vertex positions of the geometry, the surface normals, the light positions, and the base colors of the objects are all first written to textures. These textures are sampled to produce the final image. With this tech, the scene can support hundreds of dynamic lights instead of only the handful a traditional forward renderer supplies.

    • Raytracer

      • To the right, you can see a scene I made with my traditional software raytracer. Raytracing works by casting light simulating rays from the camera's perspective into the scene. The objects that intersect with the rays have their color and normals sampled. The rays then take bounce in the direction of the lights in the scene. If the rays reach a light, then intersection points earlier on surfaces are lit. Realistic, physically based rendering simulations are achieved with raytracing. My raytracer supports spheres, ellipses, bounding boxes, cylinders, reflections, and basic texturing.

    • Forward Renderer with dynamic geometry

      • Dr. Sueda, as a part of 441: Computer Graphics, had us make a traditional forward-based renderer with a free-look camera controlled with the mouse. Movement in the scene is accomplished with the WASD, as in many video games. Graphics hardware acceleration is supported through GLSL shader code. Shaders run on the GPU and operate on vertex and texture information provided to the shaders by the programmer's main application. Additionally, we experimented with dynamic geometry transformations and animations generated by GPU geometry shader code.

    • Mehen Remix

      • Mehen is one of the oldest known board games. Mehen boards have been dated to as early as 3000 BC. The rules to Mehen are lost to time. My game design team created a new game using only the traditional known Mehan pieces - a spiral board and round stone game pieces. We used these pieces to create a tactical turn-based strategy game centered around capturing a "sacred realm" on the board and gaining more followers than the rival deity.
